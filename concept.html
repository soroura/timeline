A single capacity-building rhythm that scales Emergency Care and Disaster Preparedness together, without one stealing oxygen from the other.

WHO Country Office Egypt· 2026 Concept· Editorial draft v1
The premise

Capacity isn't built by training once — it's built by a rhythm.

Standalone trainings produce graduates. They don't produce capacity. Capacity emerges when foundation, reinforcement, hospital-level practice, and recognition all run together — across enough cycles for skills to take hold.

Most national health workforce programs run one mode at a time: a wave of trainings, then a gap, then a wave of TOTs, then a gap, then maybe drills. Each wave matters, but the rhythm between them is what makes the skills stick. We're proposing a single 27-week cadence where four complementary modes run in parallel — each amplifying the others — so trainees come out the other end with shared language, shared practice, and demonstrated capability at their own hospital.

The same logic applies to Disaster Preparedness. Rather than treating it as a separate program with its own calendar, we interleave it with Emergency Care so the same six entities and twelve hospitals carry both improvements forward together.

Two programs · One rhythm

Emergency Care and Disaster Preparedness move together.

Both programs share the same six entities, the same twelve hospitals, the same training week. Trainees and trainers experience them as a single integrated effort — not two competing demands on their calendar.

Program 1

Emergency Care

Building everyday emergency-care competency across the system — from full-course BEC training to specialist process TOT to QI projects at the hospital level.

  • BEC Foundation — 8 events / month, two parallel tracks (full + hybrid)
  • EC Process TOT — alternating monthly 3-day national TOTs
  • EC Microlearning — half-day sessions at 2 hospitals × 3 cohorts
  • QI Showcase — end-of-cohort project presentation per hospital
  • EC Closing — flagship national event in December
Program 2

Disaster Preparedness

Building hospital resilience and MCM (mass-casualty management) capability through specialist trainer renewal, microlearning, and hospital-led drills.

  • D/MCM TOT — alternating monthly 3-day national TOTs
  • D Microlearning — half-day sessions at 2 hospitals × 3 cohorts
  • Hospital Drill — implementation + drill per hospital
  • D/MCM Closing — flagship national event in December

Design principles

How the rhythm is built.

Six choices that hold the model together. Each is a deliberate trade-off — not a default.

01

Parallel, not serial

All four components run at the same time. We don't wait for one wave to finish before starting the next. The rhythm comes from the overlap.

02

Foundation never stops

BEC delivery runs every week of the program. Other components layer on top — but the foundation is the constant.

03

Specialist renewal monthly

TOTs alternate by track. We refresh either Process (EC) or Disaster/MCM trainers once a month — keeping both pools active without spiking demand.

04

Hospitals carry the practice

The hardest part of training is what happens after the course. Microlearning and culmination events put the practice where the work is — at the hospital, with in-hospital champions.

05

Phase-in, not full-throttle from day one

Month 1 is for setup. No TOT yet. Hospital cohorts start in Week 3 to allow facility assessment. Foundation begins immediately, but everything else respects the readiness curve.

06

Recognition matters

The two closing events aren't ceremonial — they're contracts. Public recognition signals that the work was seen, valued, and is expected to continue past December.

What changes by December

The 6-month outcome.

The headline numbers are not the goal — they're the proof that the rhythm ran. The goal is the system change.

1,400
Trainees reached

Across both programs, six entities, twelve hospitals. Trained, retrained, and reinforced.

12
Hospitals with practice

Six hospitals can run a disaster drill. Six hospitals have a documented QI project. All twelve have in-house champions.

11
TOT cycles delivered

A renewed trainer pool — BEC, EC Process, Disaster/MCM — ready to sustain training beyond this program.

266
Training day-events

Six months in which something is happening, somewhere, every working week.
